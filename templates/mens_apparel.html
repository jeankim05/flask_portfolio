Tips for shopping

While the sports brands listed here are all renowned for their quality, finding the right product for your needs can be daunting. Here are a few tips to aid your search:
  1. Shop for the weather. Sport brands use a variety of fabrics, all of which have their pros and cons. For example, cotton is light and breathable, and it absorbs sweat. This makes it great for summer exercise, but not so much in cold areas or during winter. To find information about an item's material, you can check the tag, item description, or visit this link to learn about the different types of fabrics.
  2. Consider the need for waterproof clothing. Does your area get a lot of rain? Will you be wearing the item during or after an aquatic sport? If so, you will probably want to search for "waterproof". If the brand you're looking at doesn't have any suitable items for you, consider checking more general outdoor brands.
  3. Check each brand's sizing. Most brands have their own sizing systems, meaning that a size 6 from one brand may be a size 3 at another. It's important to know your size for each store before you buy. You can usually find sizing charts on the brand's official website or in-store.

Brand recommendations

  1. Asics is known for their running shoes, which utilize gel technology to absorb the shock every time your foot hits the ground. Their shoes are incredibly comfortable and last long.
  2. Nike, Adidas, Under Armour, and Puma are all good general brands. Under Armour in particular has great leggings that don't lose elasticity with frequent usage.
  3. As mentioned in the shopping guide, you may need to check outdoor brands for what you're looking for. I highly recommend Timberland and Columbia, as I've used their products before, and they did not disappoint.
